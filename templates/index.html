The Abyssinian

One of the oldest cat breeds in the world. Its representatives have huge expressive eyes and a beautiful textured color, for which the Abyssinians are called "sun cats". The breed's distinctive appearance, seeming long, lean, and finely colored compared to other cats, has been analogized to that of human fashion models. Personality-wise, the cats traditionally display active, curious attitudes in which they frequently follow owners around and encourage play. Their dog-like characteristics also involve a particular sense of affection and desire for interaction.

$20
Maine Coon

The king-size and thick, fluffy coat make the Maine Coon one of the most beautiful cat breeds in the world. Like other fluffy breeds, Maine Coons need good cat food, with plenty of vitamins and minerals. Maine Coons are just as loyal and friendly to people as dogs. Unlike many other domestic cats, they never require constant attention from the owner. These gentle giants adapt to any environment and are quiet yet playful. An excellent option for a home with children who already understand that you should not grab the “kitty” by the mustache and tail.

$150
Siamese cat

The Siamese cat is known for its loud voice and unique appearance. By temperament, these cats are closer to extroverts: noisy, active, sociable. They do not tolerate loneliness and boredom, so they are not suitable for those who spend little time at home. They like to spend time outdoors, they are attracted to the street. The Siamese cat comes in two distinct variations: traditional, with a rounded head and a slightly chubby body; or the modern Siamese, which are very skinny and have a wedge-shaped head.They are the more common breeds to have non-white irises.

$17
Toyger

The toyger breed is so named because its representatives look like miniature toy tigers. They weigh from 4 to 6 kilograms. It is very difficult to find toygers outside of the United States. The toy cat breed is still "in development". Unique tiger stripes are randomly distributed over the light orange torso of these cats. Toygers are muscular, have small rounded ears and a long tail. Unlike their "big brothers", they are playful, easy to train and love to interact with people.

$18
Scottish Fold

Sweetness and meekness are the main reasons for the popularity of this breed, in addition to their extravagant appearance. Frequent moving, change of environment and others do not cause panic attacks in them. Scottish folds are especially gentle with their main owner, who, when living in a family, is chosen by themselves. This breed of cats does not really like to be in the hands of a person, but they like the company of people, so they often chase the owner from room to room.

$10
Russian blue cat

Russian Blues are plush short-haired, shimmering pale blue-gray cats with emerald green eyes. Guard hairs are distinctly silver-tipped giving the cat a silvery sheen or lustrous appearance. It is a graceful yet muscular cat with a slender body, wedge-shaped head and brilliant emerald green eyes. She is an excellent rat catcher, distrustful of strangers, very affectionate and playful with children (while not releasing her claws), and behaves delicately and meekly with her owners.

$15
Burmese cat

Burmese cats are known as loving and loyal animals. This breed was specially bred as a companion cat. The Burmese are accommodating and modest, they are distinguished by sociability, intelligence and friendliness. These cats are people-oriented, curious, and have a calm and quiet demeanor. These are active, energetic animals that need human company. They love outdoor games, explore new things with interest. This breed does not need a lot of space, but it can occasionally be free-range

$12
The Nibelung

The Nibelung is a long-haired version of the Russian Blue cat. The difference between these two breeds is only in the length of the coat. And the wool of these animals is really of an unusual structure - soft, silky, does not fall off, flows, with a dense undercoat. The tips of the awns are almost discolored (have a barely noticeable blue tint), and due to the density of the wool in the sunlight, it seems that a cloudy halo or foggy cloud appears around the cat's body. A fluffy, long, slightly pointed tail in the form of a plume gives the cat sophistication

$14
The Turkish Angora

The Turkish Angora is generally a medium-sized cat. She is a muscular cat and should not appear thin or emaciated. The ears of the Turkish Angora are high, the eyes are slightly slanted. The hind legs are higher than the front. This elegant cat always looks alert, active and proud. Turkish Angora medium length. The single layered coat has no undercoat, making this longhaired cat easy to groom. The coat has seasonal variations, but even when it is shorter, it should be soft and silky and flow when she is in motion.
